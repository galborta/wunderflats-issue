## What You Can Claim

### Category 1: Rent Abatement (guaranteed under French law)
You owe zero rent for any day you cannot occupy the apartment.

- Daily rate: €2,205 ÷ 30 = **€73.50/day**
- If delivered June 15 (11 days late): **€808.50 minimum abatement**
- If delivered later: add €73.50 per additional day

### Category 2: Alternative Accommodation Costs
Fully recoverable as direct and foreseeable damages (Art. 1231-2 Cc).
- Hotel/Airbnb receipts from June 4 onward
- You contracted for a Paris apartment at €2,205/mo — a comparable standard of accommodation is reasonable, you are not required to find the cheapest option
- Keep every receipt. No exceptions.

### Category 3: Moving and Storage
- Storage if you cannot move belongings in on June 4
- Cost of moving twice (temp → apartment) if it comes to that

### Category 4: Additional Transportation
- Extra metro, taxi, Uber costs if temp accommodation is less well-located
- Save every receipt or screenshot every ride

### Category 5: Moral Damages (préjudice moral)
- Stress and disruption from finding out 3 days before move-in
- French magistrate courts: typically €300–€1,000 for straightforward cases
- More if you can document concrete impacts (work meetings disrupted, medical appointments, etc.)
