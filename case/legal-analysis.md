# Legal Analysis — Valerie Cronenbold Cespedes v. Christopher Coudray / Wunderflats

## Confidence level: HIGH on French law framework; MODERATE on specific quantum of damages

---

## 1. The Core Legal Violation

The landlord has committed **inexécution contractuelle** (contractual non-performance) under Articles 1217 and 1231-1 of the French Civil Code, and specifically violated his obligation under **Article 6 of the Loi n°89-462 du 6 juillet 1989**, which requires the lessor to deliver the rented premises in good usable condition on the agreed date.

### Why this is NOT force majeure

The landlord may try to invoke force majeure (Article 1218 Cc). This argument fails. For force majeure to apply under French law, the event must simultaneously be:

- **Imprévisible** (unforeseeable): Water damage in an existing apartment is a known, insurable risk. Every property owner is expected to carry insurance (assurance propriétaire non-occupant). This was foreseeable in the sense that it is a category of risk that landlords must plan for.
- **Irrésistible** (irresistible/unavoidable): A landlord CAN hire emergency contractors at higher cost. The delay is a consequence of cost management choices, not physical impossibility.
- **Extérieur** (external to the debtor): The apartment is the landlord's own property. The state of the apartment at delivery is entirely within his sphere of responsibility.

French jurisprudence is consistent on this: a leaking pipe and repair delay does not qualify as force majeure for a landlord's delivery obligation. The landlord bears the risk of the apartment's condition. See e.g. Cass. 3e civ., consistently holding landlords liable for non-delivery due to works/repairs.

## 2. Your Remedies Under French Law

### A. Suspension of rent
You owe zero rent for any period during which you cannot occupy the apartment. The synallagmatic nature of the lease means your payment obligation is conditional on delivery.

### B. Alternative accommodation costs (Art. 1231-2 Cc)
All direct and foreseeable losses caused by the non-delivery are recoverable:
- Hotel / Airbnb / temporary housing from June 4 onward
- Storage costs if applicable
- Double moving costs (temp housing → apartment)
- Extra transportation if temp location is inconvenient
**Keep every single receipt.**

### C. Contract termination (Art. 1217 Cc)
If the landlord cannot deliver by a date you specify in a mise en demeure, you can:
- Demand specific performance (force delivery)
- Terminate the contract at the landlord's fault
- Claim full reimbursement of all amounts paid
- Claim damages on top

### D. Moral damages (préjudice moral)
French courts award these routinely in housing cases: typically €300–€1,500 at magistrate level, more with strong documentation of disruption.

### E. Critical note on the bail mobilité
The bail mobilité is **non-renewable and non-extendable beyond 10 months**. Every day of delay the landlord causes shrinks your actual tenancy WITHOUT reducing your contractual exposure. This is additional damages: you contracted for 4 months 8 days of housing and received less. Even if the landlord delivers eventually, you have a damages claim for the lost tenancy days.

## 3. The Wunderflats Platform Angle

Wunderflats is not a neutral post-box. Under French and EU law they have active obligations:

- **EU P2B Regulation (EU 2019/1150):** Platform-to-business rules require transparency and fair dispute resolution.
- **French Code de la Consommation:** Consumer protection applies to the transaction you entered through the platform.
- **Payment escrow:** If they hold your funds, releasing them to a landlord who has not performed is a distinct liability for the platform.
- **ADR obligation:** EU ADR Directive requires platforms to direct consumers to an accredited dispute resolution scheme. Ask them explicitly which ADR body covers your dispute.
- **DGCCRF:** France's consumer protection and competition authority. A complaint here gets corporate attention fast.

The fact that they promised a French-team callback and delivered nothing in writing is itself an independent service failure.

- Do NOT cancel the contract yourself without a written full-reimbursement agreement in hand — you could be construed as the terminating party
